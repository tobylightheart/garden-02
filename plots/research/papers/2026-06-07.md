## Key Papers

### 1. TailLoR: Protecting Principal Components in Parameter-Efficient Continual Learning
- **arXiv ID:** [2606.06494v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06494v1)
- **Authors:** Marius Dragoi, Ioana Pintilie, Alexandra Dragomir, Antonio Barbalau, Florin Brad
- **Published:** 2026-06-04
- **Categories:** cs.LG
- **Abstract:** Parameter-efficient finetuning methods based on spectral decomposition have enabled progress in Continual Learning. In this paper we introduce TailLoR, which utilizes the singular bases U and V of the pre-trained weights as a fixed reference frame to learn a low-rank update applied to the singular values. This approach protects the principal components while allowing adaptation to new tasks.
- **PDF:** [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.06494v1](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.06494v1)
- **Abs:** [https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06494v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06494v1)

**Significance:** This paper addresses the core challenge of continual learning—balancing new knowledge acquisition with preservation of prior knowledge. The TailLoR approach uses spectral decomposition to protect principal components, offering a novel angle on parameter-efficient continual learning.

### 4. Regret Minimization with Adaptive Opponents in Repeated Games
- **arXiv ID:** [2606.06486v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06486v1)
- **Authors:** Mingyang Liu, Asuman Ozdaglar, Tiancheng Yu, Kaiqing Zhang
- **Published:** 2026-06-04
- **Categories:** cs.LG, cs.AI, cs.GT
- **Abstract:** In this paper, we study regret minimization in repeated games with adaptive opponents who can respond based on histories of play. The standard metric of external regret in online learning is known to fail to capture such adaptivity. To account for players' counterfactual reasoning, we propose a new metric that considers the opponent's potential responses.
- **PDF:** [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.06486v1](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.06486v1)
- **Abs:** [https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06486v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06486v1)

**Significance:** This paper directly addresses online learning in adversarial settings with adaptive opponents—a critical challenge for practical online learning applications.
