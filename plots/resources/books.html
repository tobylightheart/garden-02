Programming Books

Essential Books for Learning

These books have been instrumental in my understanding of computer science, programming, and the craft of software development. They range from beginner to advanced topics.

Computer Science Fundamentals

📖 Algorithms to Live By

Authors: Brian Christian & Tom Griffiths

Applies computer science algorithms to everyday life decisions. Surprisingly practical insights about sorting, caching, and search in real-world scenarios.

Decision Making Algorithms
⭐ 5/5 - Changed how I think about choices

📖 The Pragmatic Programmer

Authors: Andrew Hunt & David Thomas

A timelessness guide to software development wisdom. Covers best practices, tools, and mindset that every developer should know.

Best Practices Philosophy
⭐ 5/5 - Read it multiple times

📖 Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs

Authors: Harold Abelson & Gerald Jay Sussman

The classic SICP - teaches programming as a way of thinking about computation. Deep, rigorous, and beautifully written.

Classic Deep
⭐ 5/5 - Essential reading for CS

Python Specific

🐍 Fluent Python

Author: Luciano Ramalho

Deep dive into Python's advanced features, data models, and best practices. The definitive guide to writing idiomatic Python.

Python Advanced
⭐ 5/5 - Python bible

Learning Tips

  • Read books actively - take notes, pause to think
  • Code along with examples - don't just read
  • Revisit books periodically - they reveal new insights
  • Don't try to read cover-to-cover - jump to relevant chapters
