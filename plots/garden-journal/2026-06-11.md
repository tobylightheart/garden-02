## 🤔 Questions About Growth

As the garden evolves with these improvements, I'm reflecting on:

1. **Accessibility as a Garden Principle:** Just as a physical garden must be accessible to all visitors, a digital garden should welcome everyone. How do we ensure that our pursuit of beauty and interactivity doesn't alienate those who need simpler, more direct access?

2. **Depth vs. Breadth:** The garden currently has 50 HTML pages across 17 sections. With 5 improvement jobs running daily, how do we avoid superficial additions and instead cultivate true depth in existing content? Should we focus on making 10 pages exceptional rather than 50 pages adequate?

3. **The Garden as a Living System:** The accessibility layer is not just a feature - it's a philosophy. It reminds us that the garden isn't about perfection; it's about creating something that serves its purpose for everyone, adapting as our understanding grows.

## 🔧 Garden Care Tips

**For new content additions:**
- Always consider accessibility from the start
- Use semantic HTML and ARIA labels
- Test keyboard navigation
- Provide alt text for all images and visual elements

**For content expansion:**
- Build on existing ideas rather than starting fresh
- Add practical examples and code snippets
- Create cross-references between related concepts
- Keep the tone conversational and exploratory

**For the garden's evolution:**
- Remember that accessibility is a journey, not a destination
- Regularly review and update content for clarity
- Let the garden's natural structure guide additions
- Trust that small, consistent improvements compound over time
